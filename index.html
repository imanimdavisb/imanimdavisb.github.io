My Story

From community leadership to AI for social impact.

I graduated from Morehouse College with a BBA in Marketing while working full-time in Amazon operations — balancing overnight shifts, leadership roles, community service, and raising my daughter. That experience taught me how to solve real-world problems with limited resources.

Volunteering with nonprofits and mentoring students showed me something that stuck: organizations doing the most important work often have the least capacity to do it. Staff were buried in repetitive administrative tasks instead of directly helping people. That realization is what pulled me toward AI.

Today I build practical AI tools that help organizations work more efficiently, improve access to resources, and better serve their communities — combining business strategy, technology, and a genuine commitment to impact.

Claude Community Resource Navigator

The Problem

Community organizations spend significant time identifying client needs and matching them to local resources — delaying assistance and adding administrative burden to already stretched teams.

What I Built

A Python application that guides users through an intake process, identifies their needs, asks targeted follow-up questions, and recommends relevant resources — producing organized summaries staff can act on immediately.

What I Learned

Building useful AI tools isn't just about generating answers — it's about asking the right questions and designing experiences that make life easier for both staff and community members.

If I Rebuilt This Today

I would integrate live community resource databases, improve the intake workflow with more personalized follow-up questions, and build a web application so nonprofit staff could use it from any device without installing Python.

Eminent Wellness Support Assistant

The Problem

Counseling organizations spend valuable appointment time collecting basic intake information before any support can begin.

What I Built

An AI wellness intake assistant that gathers client concerns, summarizes information, and recommends next steps — while clearly communicating it does not replace professional care.

What I Learned

AI works best when it supports professionals rather than replacing them. Responsible design is part of the build, not an afterthought.

If I Rebuilt This Today

I would add multilingual support, appointment scheduling, secure staff dashboards, and additional accessibility features to make the tool more useful for both clients and counselors.

Claude Personal Finance Dashboard

The Problem

Many people struggle to understand where their money goes each month, making it hard to budget or build savings.

What I Built

A Python dashboard tracking income, expenses, budgets, savings goals, and monthly trends — with easy-to-read summaries that turn raw data into clear decisions.

What I Learned

Even simple dashboards create real impact when information is presented clearly. Accessibility of data matters as much as accuracy.

If I Rebuilt This Today

I would build a web-based dashboard with interactive charts, recurring expense tracking, bank account integration, and AI-powered budgeting recommendations personalized to each user's financial goals.
